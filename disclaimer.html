DATA ACCURACY DISCLAIMER

Atom Registry retrieves all blockchain data from public REST endpoints operated by third-party node providers including PublicNode, Cosmos Directory, and chain-specific public nodes. We do not operate these nodes and have no control over the data they return.

KNOWN LIMITATIONS

  • Latency — Data displayed reflects the state of the network at the time of the most recent query, which may be seconds to minutes behind the actual chain head
  • Node disagreements — Different nodes may return slightly different data during periods of high load, upgrades, or network stress
  • Finality — Blocks and transactions displayed as confirmed may in rare cases be subject to reorganization, particularly on chains with longer finality windows
  • Endpoint availability — Public nodes may be unavailable, rate-limited, or returning stale data at any given time; Atom Registry attempts failover but cannot guarantee data availability
  • Governance data — Tally data for active proposals is updated at query time and may not reflect votes cast in the final seconds before a voting period closes
  • Price data — Prices are sourced from CoinGecko and may differ from prices on specific exchanges; they are indicative only
  • IBC denom traces — Denom resolution relies on trace data from connected chains, which may be incomplete or temporarily unavailable

Do not use data from Atom Registry as the sole basis for any time-sensitive or financially significant decision. Always verify critical information using multiple independent sources, including direct node queries or block explorers operated by the chain teams themselves.

VALIDATOR AND STAKING DISCLAIMER

Atom Registry displays validator information including voting power, commission rates, uptime estimates, and self-delegation amounts. This information is presented as-is from on-chain data and does not constitute an endorsement or recommendation of any validator.

  • Uptime bars show a limited historical window and do not predict future performance
  • Validators may change their commission rates at any time on-chain
  • A validator's displayed status (bonded, unbonding, tombstoned) reflects the most recent queried state and may not be current

Staking decisions should be made after independent research. Consider factors including validator security practices, track record, team transparency, and your own risk tolerance.
